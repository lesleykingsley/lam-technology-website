About LAM Technology

The advisor you keep.

An executive who hired LAM has changed companies and brought us with them 32 times. Ten of those executives have brought LAM to a third company. In a market where leadership turns over every three to five years, that is the only metric that tells you whether the work was worth keeping. It is also the most honest answer we can give to the question of why LAM.

Where We Sit

Supplier-neutral
We carry every relevant supplier and we charge for our own work. That combination is what keeps the recommendation honest — no one supplier owns our paycheck, and our advice isn’t free, so it isn’t biased toward whoever is.
Why It Happens

Three reasons clients take us with them.

It's never the same reason twice on the surface. Underneath, it's almost always one of these.

01

The work is theirs to point to.

When LAM finishes an engagement, the executive who hired us has receipts — recovered spend, renegotiated contracts, a cleaner supplier roster, a roadmap their board can read. They take credit, because they should. We don't.

02

We don't sell what we can't stand behind.

Supplier-neutral isn't a slogan. It's a structural commitment that means we sometimes recommend nothing, sometimes recommend the supplier you already have, and sometimes recommend the one with the smaller logo. Executives notice when their consultant says no on their behalf.

03

We operate as part of the team.

Joint business reviews with your suppliers, with us sitting on your side of the table. Renewal advocacy that runs months ahead of contract end. The kind of working relationship that makes the executive's next employer say "bring them along."
