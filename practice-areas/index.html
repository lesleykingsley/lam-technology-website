Five Lenses.

LAM doesn't sell features. We bring focused expertise to the technology questions mid-market IT leaders actually face — and we run every practice the same way: a branded assessment to set the baseline, a clear methodology for the work, and a path that flows back into the engagement model.

How Practice Areas Work at LAM

Each lens runs on the same operating principles.

A practice area is more than a list of services. It's a defined way of looking at a part of your business that travels with you through the four-phase engagement model.

01 — Uncover
Every practice opens with a branded assessment.
No engagement starts with a recommendation. Each lens has a defined assessment that establishes the baseline, surfaces the real questions, and gives you something concrete to act on — whether or not the next step is with us.
02 — Operate
A clear methodology, not a billable hours sprawl.
Every practice has a structured approach with named domains, defined deliverables, and a fixed scope. You know what you're getting, what you're paying for, and what you'll have at the end — before we start.
03 — Steward
Findings flow into the engagement model.
Practice area work doesn't end at the report. Findings inform the strategy phase, shape the execution path, and stay with you through ongoing support. That's what makes the work compound over time.

Networking & Infrastructure · Infrastructure Insight™
For leaders inheriting infrastructure that quietly costs them every day.
Is my infrastructure helping or holding the business back?
Infrastructure rarely fails dramatically — it erodes. Aging gear, drifting MSP relationships, ISP contracts no one's reviewed in five years, and device lifecycle programs that just keep billing. This lens delivers a practical, prioritized read on where your infrastructure and the suppliers running pieces of it actually stand — and what's worth changing first.

Customer Experience · Experience Examination™
For leaders whose customer experience vision keeps colliding with their technology reality.

A clear-eyed look at how customers actually move through your business across voice, digital, and self-service channels. We surface the friction points that are quietly costing engagement, loyalty, and revenue — and give you a defensible plan for where to focus.

Mobility & IoT · Wireless Wisdom™

What is wireless really costing us — in dollars and in capability?
